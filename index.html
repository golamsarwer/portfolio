About Me

I am an Information Management and Data professional with over five years of hands-on experience in humanitarian and development settings. Why "clean data" is a life-or-death issue in refugee response

During my time at UNOPS/UNHCR in Cox's Bazar, I learned that a single duplicate in a refugee registration system can mean a family misses their food distribution. A wrong GPS coordinate in an ArcGIS service map can send an emergency responder to the wrong camp.

Data quality in humanitarian settings isn't a technical nicety — it's operational integrity. Every ETL step I design, every SQL validation I run, I think about that family waiting. 🏕️

GIS in humanitarian response: mapping lives, not just geography

After completing my 3-month GIS & Remote Sensing certification in June 2025, I reflected on what spatial analysis really means in a camp environment.

When you overlay a flood-risk layer onto a shelter distribution map for a Rohingya camp, you're not making a pretty map — you're identifying which families need to be relocated before the monsoon hits. 🗺️

ArcGIS and QGIS are tools. The value comes from asking the right question.

Power BI changed how donors see field data — here's how

Before dashboards, our donor reporting was: Excel → PDF → email. Updates were monthly. Decision-making lagged.

After implementing a live Power BI dashboard connected to our KoboToolbox data at BRAC HCMP, programme coordinators could see shelter status, WASH coverage, and protection gaps — updated weekly, filterable by camp block.

Data literacy + the right tool = faster decisions. 📊 Happy to share lessons if you're building similar systems.

Climate Data · FAO Bangladesh

What working on Bangladesh's GHG inventory taught me about data governance

At FAO, I helped maintain Bangladesh's national Greenhouse Gas database — data that feeds directly into UNFCCC reporting and GCF funding decisions.

What I learned: the challenge isn't collecting the data. It's standardisation across 15 ministries, each with different formats, units, and fiscal years.

Good data governance frameworks are invisible when they work — and catastrophic when they don't. 🌿
