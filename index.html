Who I Am

About Me

I am an Information Management and Data professional with over five years of hands-on experience in humanitarian and development settings. My career spans some of the most challenging and rewarding environments — refugee response in Cox's Bazar, national climate data systems at FAO, and refugee registration with UNHCR.

What drives me is the belief that good data leads to better decisions — and better decisions save lives. I bridge the gap between raw, messy field data and the clean, structured insights that donors, governments, and field teams need to act.

With a BSc in Computer Science & Engineering and an ongoing MSc, I bring both a technical engineering mindset and a humanitarian practitioner's sensitivity to every project I take on.

🗂️ Information Systems

End-to-end IM frameworks, KPI trackers, service maps, and data archives for refugee response in Cox's Bazar — supporting over one million Rohingya across the camps.

📊 Data Visualization

Power BI, ArcGIS, SSRS — transforming messy field data into clean, decision-ready dashboards and maps for UN agencies, donors, and government partners.

🌱 Climate & Environment

Managed Bangladesh's national GHG inventory database at FAO, supporting UNFCCC reporting and the Green Climate Fund (GCF) programme.

💡 Digital Innovation
